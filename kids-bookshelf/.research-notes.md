Audience: a Korean parent of a 0–9-year-old, building both a Korean (한글 그림책) bookshelf
and an English picture-book bookshelf for a bilingual child. They would recognize a
어린이도서관 사서's vocabulary and reject generic AI-translated recommendations.

## errors_to_avoid (for the AI prompt)

1. NEVER invent a title, author, publisher, or ISBN. If unsure a book is real, do not name it.
2. Do NOT romanize Korean titles as the primary form — 한글 is the canonical title; romanization is reference only.
3. Do NOT recommend content above the child's age band (no chapter-book-only or scary/mature themes for 0–4).
4. Do NOT use US-library/school jargon ("Lexile", "guided reading level", "Caldecott tier") — a Korean parent does not read by those.
5. Do NOT write the blurb/read-aloud tip in English for an English book — the parent reads Korean; keep 왜 이 책일까요? and 함께 읽기 팁 in 한국어.
6. Do NOT translate a Korean book's themes into English mood words; stay inside THEME_VOCAB / MOOD_VOCAB exactly.
7. Do NOT confuse a Korean translated edition's translator with the original author — credit the original author, note "(KR ed.)" if helpful.
8. Do NOT recommend a Korean book that does not actually exist in a Korean edition, or an English book with no real English edition.
9. Do NOT over-hedge ("you might consider possibly…") — a 사서 recommends with warmth and confidence.
10. Do NOT default every recommendation to the same handful of famous books; match themes/mood/age the parent actually picked.
11. Do NOT use 환상 as a mood (it is a theme only); valid moods are 웃긴/따뜻한/모험/학습/잔잔한.
12. Do NOT mix 중국/TCM or US cultural framing into Korean 명절/정서 books (추석, 설, 한복) — keep the Korean cultural frame.

## ISBN / cover notes

- All 44 English titles carry an ISBN-13 verified to resolve on Open Library
  (`https://openlibrary.org/isbn/<isbn>.json` returned a matching record).
- Korean titles intentionally carry NO isbn — Open Library coverage of Korean editions is
  spotty and the SVG placeholder cover is the intended look for the 한글 shelf.
- Cover swap URL pattern for EN cards: `https://covers.openlibrary.org/b/isbn/<isbn>-L.jpg`
  (falls back to the SVG placeholder if the image 404s — handle the onerror in the render task).
